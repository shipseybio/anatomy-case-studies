Definitions

  • Saddle Anesthesia: Numbness in the inner thighs, groin, and buttocks.
  • Urinary Retention: Difficulty or inability to fully empty the bladder.
  • Red Flag Symptoms: Urgent symptoms requiring immediate evaluation.
  • Sciatica: Radiating leg pain caused by compression of the sciatic nerve.

Patient Overview

John, a 42-year-old warehouse worker, has had occasional sciatica for years. He now presents with 3 days of intense back pain and left leg numbness after lifting a heavy box. He describes a "pop" in his back and has since felt tingling in his groin and weakness in his left leg. He is struggling with urination and cannot sit comfortably due to pain.

Saddle Anesthesia

John's symptoms include saddle anesthesia, which is characterized by numbness in the inner thighs, groin, and buttocks. This is an important indicator of possible spinal nerve involvement in conditions like Cauda Equina Syndrome.

Task: Review the Case

Read the patient's history and review the symptoms described above. Pay particular attention to key symptoms like sciatica, leg numbness, and urinary issues.

Question 1:

Which of the following is a symptom that may indicate a spinal nerve issue?

  • A. Shortness of breath
  • B. Pain radiating into a leg
  • C. Blurry vision
  • D. Sneezing

Answer: B. Pain radiating into a leg
