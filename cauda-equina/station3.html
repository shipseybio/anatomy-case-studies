Definitions

  • Two-Point Discrimination: The ability to detect two points as separate stimuli.
  • Dermatome: A skin area innervated by one spinal nerve.

Task: Sensory Testing

One way to assess nerve damage is to use calipers to perform two-point discrimination testing. Compare John’s result to the normal range chart. Also, use a dermatome map to help identify the affected spinal nerve root.

Patient Overview

During the sensory exam, John is asked "One touch or two?". He can accurately tell the difference on his arm and fingers. But he demonstrates loss of feeling on his left lateral calf and foot, along with saddle anesthesia.

Two-Point Discrimination Testing

Two-point discrimination testing on the lateral calf shows a result of 65mm, which is abnormal when compared to normal ranges.
